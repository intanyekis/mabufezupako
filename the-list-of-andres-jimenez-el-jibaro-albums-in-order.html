The List of Andres Jimenez “El Jibaro” Albums in Order

Andres Jimenez, widely known as “El Jibaro,” was a Puerto Rican singer and composer who became one of the most influential figures in the Nueva Canción genre. Over his career that spanned several decades, El Jibaro released numerous albums, each showcasing his unique style and powerful voice.

Here is a comprehensive list of Andres Jimenez “El Jibaro” albums presented in chronological order:

1. “Mi Tierra y Yo” (1965) – This debut album by El Jibaro established him as a prominent voice in Puerto Rican folk music, featuring songs that celebrated the island’s rich cultural heritage.

2. “Por Aqui, Por Alla” (1967) – With this album, El Jibaro expanded his repertoire, incorporating diverse musical styles while staying true to his roots. The album resonated with listeners and solidified his popularity.

3. “Paloma Errante” (1970) – El Jibaro showcased his versatility in this album, combining traditional Puerto Rican jibaro music with contemporary influences. The title track, “Paloma Errante,” became an instant hit.

4. “Por el Resto de Mis Dias” (1973) – Known for his touching and heartfelt ballads, El Jibaro released this album, which included songs that explored themes of love, longing, and the beauty of life.

5. “Caras Lindas” (1978) – This album became a milestone in El Jibaro’s career, as it tackled social and political issues that resonated with the Puerto Rican community. The album featured the popular song “Caras Lindas,” known for its uplifting message about racial pride.

6. “En Mi Viejo San Juan” (1982) – El Jibaro paid homage to his beloved hometown, San Juan, with this album. It consisted of sentimental songs that showcased his deep connection to the history and culture of Puerto Rico.

7. “Cuentos de Mi Tierra” (1987) – In this album, El Jibaro delved into storytelling, taking listeners on a musical journey through Puerto Rico’s folklore and traditions. The album celebrated the island’s rich heritage and solidified El Jibaro’s status as a cultural icon.

8. “Alma de Poeta” (1991) – El Jibaro collaborated with renowned poets and lyricists in this album, setting their words to music and creating a fusion of poetry and song that captivated audiences.

9. “Aleluya” (1995) – This gospel album showcased El Jibaro’s spiritual side, featuring religious songs that demonstrated his vocal range and emotional depth.

10. “Homenaje a Rafael Hernandez” (2000) – El Jibaro paid tribute to the legendary Puerto Rican composer Rafael Hernandez with this album, interpreting some of his most beloved compositions in his own unique style.

11. “Navidad Jibara” (2003) – El Jibaro celebrated the holiday season with this festive album, offering traditional Christmas songs infused with his signature flair.

12. “Jibaro Hasta El Hueso” (2008) – In his final studio album, El Jibaro returned to his musical roots, showcasing the essence of jibaro music and leaving a lasting legacy.
